01 · About the work

Before you decide.

What this coaching actually is, who it's for, and how it's different from what you've tried before.

Is this only for Christian women?

No. My faith anchors my work, but the framework I teach is built on identity psychology, neuroscience, and inner healing principles that are universal. Women of every background and belief have come through this process and found it transformational.

If you're a woman of faith, you'll find me unapologetic about mine. If you're not, you won't feel preached at — you'll feel met where you are.

What's the difference between coaching and therapy?

Therapy often focuses on diagnosing, processing, and healing the past. Coaching moves you forward from where you are now.

In our work, I acknowledge what happened to you, help you understand how it formed your identity, and then we actively rebuild that identity from the inside out. Many of my clients work with both a therapist AND me — coaching for the forward movement, therapy for the deep clinical processing.

Important: I am not a licensed therapist, and coaching is not a substitute for clinical mental health support. If you're in crisis, please reach out to a licensed mental health professional.

How is your work different from other identity or life coaches?

Most coaches will tell you to change your behavior. Set a boundary. Try harder. I don't believe that works long-term, because the behavior isn't the problem — the identity underneath it is.

My framework, the Seven Fractured Identities, names the specific survival selves women learned to become. We don't change behavior at the surface. We change it at the root. That's the difference.

Do I need to have it figured out before we start?

Not even close. Most women who come to me know something feels off but can't name it. They've read the books, done the work, tried the routines, and still feel like they're circling the same mountain.

That's where this work begins. Bring the confusion. We'll work with it together.

What if I've already done a lot of healing work?

Most women I work with have done years of healing — therapy, books, conferences, retreats. They come to me when surface work has hit a ceiling.

The Seven Fractured Identities framework goes beneath the surface to the subconscious programming that's still running. Even women who've been in healing work for decades discover new clarity in this process.
